# Conflict, Repair and Forgiveness

## Repair Sequence
1. Stop escalation.
2. State observable facts.
3. Name impact without exaggeration.
4. Hear the other account.
5. Allocate responsibility proportionately.
6. Apologize specifically.
7. Repair tangible damage.
8. Define changed behavior.
9. Retest.
10. Close or escalate.

## Good Apology
Names the act, acknowledges impact, avoids “but,” offers repair, and changes future behavior.

## Forgiveness
Forgiveness is not forced reconciliation, restored access, erased memory, or immunity from consequences.

## Apology Inflation
Repeated apologies without changed behavior reduce the Repair Score.
