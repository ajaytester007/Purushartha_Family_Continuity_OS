# Life Vision Charter

## Purpose
The life vision belongs first to the person living it and, after commitment, to the jointly authored family system. No partner owns another person's future. Shared plans exist by continuing consent.

## Protected Domains
Every participant retains:
- bodily autonomy;
- privacy and dignity;
- freedom from coercion and violence;
- access to personal support;
- a voice in major financial and household decisions;
- the right to revise goals;
- the right to leave a relationship;
- the right to grief, recovery, and a life after loss.

## Shared Vision Canvas
Define:
1. What kind of people do we want to become?
2. What kind of home atmosphere do we want?
3. Which obligations are sacred?
4. What forms of prosperity matter?
5. What gives us joy?
6. What does spiritual alignment mean to us?
7. Who depends on us?
8. What risks could destabilize the household?
9. What would we preserve during a crisis?
10. What legacy should remain after us?

## Constitutional Floors
The system may optimize above these floors but not trade them away:
- physical safety;
- basic dignity;
- truthful disclosure of material facts;
- child and dependent welfare;
- financial solvency planning;
- access to health care;
- freedom from coercive control.

## Amendment Rule
Major changes require:
- explicit proposal;
- impact review;
- time to reflect;
- voluntary agreement;
- documented review date.

Silence is not consent. Exhaustion is not agreement. Financial dependence does not erase rights.
