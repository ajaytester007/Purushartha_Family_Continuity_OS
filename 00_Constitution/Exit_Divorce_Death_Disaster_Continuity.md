# Exit, Divorce, Death and Disaster Continuity

## Operating Modes
1. Flourish
2. Consolidate
3. Defend
4. Survive
5. Recover
6. Renew
7. Expand

## First 72 Hours of Shock
Prioritize:
- physical safety;
- children/dependents;
- medication and health needs;
- housing;
- liquidity;
- essential communications;
- legal or emergency help where needed.

## First 90 Days
Freeze irreversible nonessential decisions when feasible. Inventory obligations, reduce complexity, establish support, protect records, and create a temporary budget.

## Interregnum
When no partner is present, redistribute:
- emotional support;
- childcare;
- household work;
- finances;
- transportation;
- pet care;
- social connection.

The goal is graceful scale-down without abandoning the life vision.

## Recovery Exit Criteria
Move from Survive to Recover when essentials are stable. Move from Recover to Renew when grief or shock no longer dominates most decisions and future planning becomes possible.
