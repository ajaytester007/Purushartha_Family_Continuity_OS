# Book 17 — Reverse Aging and the Second Spring

## Scope
This book governs strength, mobility, sleep, nutrition, learning, grooming, curiosity, intimacy, travel and renewed purpose.

## Core Questions
1. What is changing in the individual, couple, household, and wider family?
2. Which expectations are explicit and which are assumed?
3. What burdens are temporary, recurring, or structural?
4. Who benefits and who pays?
5. What is the earliest useful intervention?
6. What would success look like in 30 days, one year, and the next life stage?

## Required Assessments
- safety floor;
- maturity and accountability;
- trust and repair;
- burden skew;
- quality of life;
- Dharma/Artha/Kama/Moksha balance;
- support network;
- financial and health resilience.

## Encounter Record
For every meaningful event record:
Fact → Context → Expectation → Conduct → Impact → Responsibility → Repair → Learning → Retest → Outcome.

## Tollgates
Continue when safety, reciprocity, and learning are intact.
Correct when the problem is bounded and teachable.
Reconsider when harm repeats or one person carries chronic disproportionate cost.
Exit or seek urgent protection when safety is compromised.

## Milestone Outcomes
- reduced unresolved burden;
- improved clarity;
- visible follow-through;
- protected joy;
- sustainable resources;
- lower recurrence of the same injury;
- preserved individual dignity.

## Blindspots
Do not confuse social appearance with health, sacrifice with consent, endurance with success, intensity with intimacy, or wealth with flourishing.

## Practice
Choose one live issue in this domain. Complete an event log, run one scorecard, select one intervention, and set a retest date.
