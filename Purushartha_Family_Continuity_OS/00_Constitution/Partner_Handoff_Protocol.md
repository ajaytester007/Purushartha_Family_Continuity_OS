# Partner Handoff Protocol

## Principle
Continuity can be handed off. A human being cannot.

## When Used
After ethical closure or irreversible loss: breakup, divorce, bereavement, long-term disappearance, displacement, or other legitimate transition.

## Continuity Brief
A new partner may receive, with appropriate consent:
- current dependents and care commitments;
- household operating realities;
- financial obligations disclosed at the appropriate stage;
- traditions and values;
- known triggers described without unnecessary intimate detail;
- lessons learned;
- active risks;
- future aspirations.

## Never Automatically Transfer
- intimate messages with a former partner;
- sexual history beyond relevant consensual disclosure;
- therapy notes;
- private voice/video archives;
- third-party secrets;
- a predecessor's score.

## Constitutional Renegotiation Window
The incoming partner can accept, reject, modify, or add:
- lifestyle model;
- financial arrangements;
- caregiving scope;
- traditions;
- geography;
- family boundaries;
- future children;
- pet obligations;
- career exposure;
- risk tolerance.

## Success Criterion
The new relationship preserves valuable continuity while creating a genuinely new shared authorship.
