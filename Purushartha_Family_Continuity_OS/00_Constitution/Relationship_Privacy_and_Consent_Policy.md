# Relationship Privacy and Consent Policy

## Data Classes
**Class A — Shared operational data:** calendars, budgets, chores, agreed goals.
**Class B — Personal relational data:** messages voluntarily submitted by the sender/recipient.
**Class C — Highly sensitive data:** health, intimacy, therapy, location history, biometrics.
**Class D — Third-party data:** friends, children, relatives, colleagues.
**Class E — Safety evidence:** threats, stalking, coercion, violence.

## Rules
- Collect the minimum needed.
- State the purpose before ingestion.
- Do not secretly record or scrape.
- Do not infer guilt from deleted data or silence.
- Do not expose one partner's private archive to a future partner.
- Summarize lessons for continuity; do not hand over intimate raw history.
- Children receive heightened protection.
- Health data cannot be used to shame or reduce a person's moral score.
- Models must label fact, allegation, inference, uncertainty, and contradiction separately.

## Consent Ledger
For each source record:
- owner;
- participants;
- collector;
- purpose;
- consent basis;
- allowed analyses;
- allowed viewers;
- retention period;
- deletion status.

## Revocation
Revocation stops future optional processing. Safety, legal, and record-retention obligations may require separate professional guidance.

## AI Rule
AI may detect patterns and generate questions. It must not declare that a person is abusive, deceptive, mentally ill, unfaithful, or dangerous solely from probabilistic signals.
